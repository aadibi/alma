Mary Dutcher

Immigration Attorney

Mary Dutcher has been practicing as an immigration attorney for twelve years. She is a member of the American Immigration Lawyers Association and the Bay Area Coalition for Immigrant Victims of Crime. In accordance with her long term commitment to public interest work Mary continues to serve at a variety of organizations dedicated to social justice. For the last eight years Mary has served on the board of directors, and is presently Treasurer, of Capacitar International, a nonprofit organization working with victims of trauma in more than 25 countries. Additionally, for the past ten years Mary has served at the National Farm Worker Ministry, which supports farm workers organizing for justice in the United States.

Reema Rahmatullah

Immigration Attorney

Reema was born in Karachi, Pakistan. She received her undergraduate degree at the University of California at Berkeley and then went on to complete her doctorate at the University of Santa Clara School of Law. Reema's concentration is in public interest work and social justice. While at Santa Clara, Reema interned at Community Legal Services of East Palo Alto to help low-income clientele get assistance with immigration issues. After graduation she worked as a Volunteer Attorney at the Katherine and George Alexander Community Law Center Immigration Clinic in San Jose, CA and also did pro bono work for victims of domestic violence.
